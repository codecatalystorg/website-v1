Where it all began...


CodeCatalyst began as a small local initiative sparked by a group of passionate high school students and their mentors in Ontario, Canada. Frustrated by the lack of accessible coding education and driven by a desire to foster technological skills among their peers, they organized small workshops and coding sessions whether that would be in person or online. These initial gatherings quickly grew in popularity, revealing a significant demand for structured and supportive learning environments. In response, the group formalized their efforts, establishing CodeCatalyst as a non-profit organization. Our mission: to empower students with skills in coding, web development, and engineering, and to inspire the next generation of tech innovators. We plan on doing so by leveraging partnerships with local schools, tech companies, and community leaders to expanded our reach, allowing us to host hackathons, mentorship programs, and online courses, all dedicated to creating opportunities for youth to thrive in the ever-evolving digital world.
